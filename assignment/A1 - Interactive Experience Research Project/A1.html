Interactive Experience Study: Rich Brown's Personal Website

Interactive Media (OART1013) | Aug, 2025

1. First Impression

This website has a visually appealing loading page, using a grey-toned turquoise background that gives a modern, clean, simple, and pleasing impression. On both the top and bottom of the loading screen, rapidly flashing capital letters appear. After entering the homepage, I realized it is the personal website of a UI/UX designer, Rich Brown. A stylish, cinematic moving-shot video of him appears in person. The flashing letters from the loading page turn out to be composed of the letters in his name.

2. Experience & Discrete Actions

  • Scrolled up and down the homepage
  • Clicked on a case study to view further details
  • Clicked on the letter “B” in the bottom-right corner
  • Clicked on the elliptical white button
  • Browsed the whole page briefly

3. Most Time-spent Parts

I was most attracted to his case studies. Each one features a looping thumbnail video directly on the main page—especially the project VIILLAINY.

4. Frequent Action

Scrolling through the homepage and clicking into case studies.

5. Primary Goal

The website aims to attract potential clients or employers, showcase his accomplishments so far, visualize an interactive portfolio, and also function a bit like a short autobiography.

6. Goal Communication

This is achieved through presenting his design cases, awards, individual artistic style, working process, and personality.

7. Impressions of Time-based Interaction

It feels like something to explore briefly in just a couple of minutes. At first, I wasn't very interested in the large blocks of text, but then I noticed some interesting design features—for example, when scrolling up and down, the text morphs with the scrolling direction.

8. Conveyed Time-Based Pattern

The letter “B” in the bottom-right corner is actually an interactive button leading to a Q&A dialogue. In this section, he explains why the site was designed to be difficult to read:

“It's a bit of an eye strain, but most people aren't reading this stuff. It's why I've put so much effort into the visuals—that's what you're really here for.”

I think this is very well thought out. It shows he is confident in his design, and overall, my experience turned out to be really good. The text on the site works more as a decorative element than an informative one. This makes me feel that he is both confident and truly excellent in his designs.

9. Referenced Media Forms

  • Product demo videos
  • Self-introduction background video
  • Magazine & portfolio slide layouts

10. References Suggest About How to Act

They guide and encourage users to explore actively:

  • Product demo videos - present design outcomes dynamically
  • Self-introduction video - introduce himself and highlight his personality
  • Magazine & portfolio slides - navigate the curated layout as if flipping through pages

11. References Suggest About How to Feel

They aim to make viewers:

  • Feel confident in his skills through curated demo videos
  • Develop a personal connection through the recorded self-introduction video
  • Feel aesthetically pleased through the clean, magazine-style layout design

12. Most Frustrating Part

One frustrating aspect was that the high-resolution images and videos were too large, which made them slow to load and disrupted the reading flow. Another frustration was the navigation bar. It sometimes felt too minimal, making it hard to locate quickly.

13. Most Satisfying Part

The most satisfying elements were the micro-interactions and storytelling flow, which made the site feel authentic and approachable. The smooth hover animations on the case study videos created a sense of polish and engagement. Each case study unfolded like a clear narrative journey—starting with the problem and demand, moving through the design process, and ending with visual display results.
